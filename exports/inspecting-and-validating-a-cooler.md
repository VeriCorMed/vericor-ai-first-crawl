_Longevity_ ‚Äì A lifetime.  
_Strengths_ ‚Äì Durability and features specifically made for transport (straps, wheels, handles, pockets, depending on model).

####  **Temperature Controlling System** (the PCM panels)  

_Longevity_ ‚Äì 10,000+ thermal cycles.  
_Inspecting_ ‚Äì Visually check for leaks and/or dirt.  
_Strengths_ ‚Äì Simple design, quality of PCM, and durability.  
_Weakness_ ‚Äì Knowing what size of panels goes in what case (when you have multiple systems).  
_Maintenance_ ‚Äì Clean using warm water and soap. Sanitization can be performed using an isopropyl alcohol and water mixture (typically 70/30) or other salt-based disinfectants. DO NOT: autoclave; expose to extreme heat (above 75¬∞C/167¬∞F); use abrasive cleaners; use acetone (or other similar solvents).
_More about the temperature controlling system‚Ä¶_  
![Fridge-Temps-PCM-System-for-Cool-Cube‚Ñ¢-03-\(6-Panels\)-CC-PCMS-BV03](https://www.vericormed.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/12/fridge-temps-pcm-system-for-cool-cube-03-6-panels-cc-pcms-bv03-150x150.png) All of the various types and sizes of phase change material (PCM) panels have been thoroughly tested (gravity, pressure, freeze/thaw, pull, etc.) to perform for years and years. The PCM itself is very pure, has a strategic melting/freezing point, is safe, non-toxic and reusable. But before panels are filled with quality PCM, they undergo a proprietary sealing process that insures material compatibility (between the PCM and plastic panel) and minimizes permeation (which all plastics exhibit). The result is a less than 0.6% overall loss of PCM over the course of ten years‚Äîwhich has been calculated to NOT significantly impact the performance of a PCM system. If you lose a panel or want to replace a set, they can be purchased √† la carte [here](https://www.vericormed.com/small-medium-large-cool-cube-components/).
####  **Insulation** (the VIP system)
_Longevity_ ‚Äì 5+ years.  
_Inspecting_ ‚Äì Visually check for a compromised panel and/or dirt. (Note: the clear polycarbonate is a protective encapsulation that does not affect the VIP performance. If the polycarbonate is compromised [cracked, punctured, or broken], it does not necessarily mean the insulation has been as well. If the VIP still holds a vacuum, taping or altering the polycarbonate is acceptable, as it is only for protection.)  
_Strengths_ ‚Äì The R-value‚Ä¶the best type of insulator AND it‚Äôs on all six sides of the cooler.  
_Weakness_ ‚Äì Could be punctured, which would decrease performance.  
_Maintenance_ ‚Äì Handle with care. Clean using warm water and soap. Sanitization can be performed using an isopropyl alcohol and water mixture (typically 70/30) or other salt-based disinfectants. DO NOT: autoclave; expose to extreme heat (above 75¬∞C/167¬∞F); use abrasive cleaners; use acetone (or other similar solvents).
_More about the VIP‚Ä¶_  
![Temp-Shield‚Ñ¢-Insulation-System-of-Cool-Cube‚Ñ¢-03-\(Complete-Set\)-CC-VIP-03](https://www.vericormed.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/12/temp-shield-insulation-system-of-cool-cube-03-complete-set-cc-vip-03-150x150.png) VeriCor‚Äôs Cool Cube‚Ñ¢ PCM coolers incorporate the use of high R-value vacuum insulation panel (VIP) assemblies. Cooler specific assemblies are comprised of six individually sealed panels. Each panel is encapsulated in a 5 mil to 10 mil polycarbonate that provides exceptional impact protection (2X that of PVC). After each panel is encapsulated, they get taped together to form the six-sided assembly.
The internal vacuum pressure gives the panel its extraordinary thermal performance. Due to the pressure difference between the inside and the outside of the panel, gas and water vapor molecules permeate through the barrier film and result in a rise of the internal vacuum pressure within the panel. Since thermal performance is directly related to the internal pressure, an aging affect is seen. All VIPs, regardless of makeup, will be subjected to this process because there are no perfect barriers that can be used (all VIPs will eventually return to atmospheric pressure). Data sets show that the average increase in thermal conductivity rose 5.15% after 60 months (5 years). Ultimately the user may continue to use aged VIP assemblies if still meeting minimum time requirements.
Inspecting each of the panels is paramount, as one compromised panel can affect the entire system. There are two ways to determine whether a panel is compromised:
  1.     1. ![VIP inspection](https://www.vericormed.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/VIP-inspection-300x233.png)Visually check the skin of the panel (white in older models; silver in newer models) as it should be sucked tight to the core creating a rippled, textured surface. If it is, it still has its vacuum and is good. If the skin has a wave or two and appears loose, the panel has been compromised and will not be as effective.
    2. Touching a panel can also tell a lot. Pushing on a good VIP panel, it will seem very rigid and hard. Pushing on a panel that has lost its vacuum will seem soft and squishy. 


Inspecting and testing of the entire system periodically, and even more frequently after the first 5 years. Upon testing, if the unit is performing up to requirements, it is fine to use. If it is not holding the temperatures as long as expected, it is likely a VIP panel (or panels) have lost its high R-value due to a vacuum loss. Replace the VIP (found [here](https://www.vericormed.com/small-medium-large-cool-cube-components/)) and you likely will have a whole new system once again.
**Cool Cube‚Ñ¢ PCM coolers protect human lives through payload protection, are a sustainable solution in regards to environmental stewardship, and have durable, long-lasting components that drive down the cost per use. Here‚Äôs to a long, protective life. ü•Ç**
